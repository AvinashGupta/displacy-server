About displaCy

displaCy lets you peek inside spaCy's syntactic parser, as it reads a sentence word-by-word. By repeatedly choosing from a small set of actions, the parser links the words together according to their syntactic structure. The four actions are:

  • Shift: Move the current focus word onto the stack, and advance the focus.
  • Reduce: Pop the word on top of the stack. If it does not have an incoming arc, it becomes the focus word.
  • Left-arc: Add an arc from the focus word to the word on top of the stack. The stack is then popped.
  • Right-arc: Add an arc from the word on top of the stack to the focus word. Move the focus word onto the stack, and advance the focus.
  • You can press spacebar to get the spaCy parser’s prediction, or hit backspace to undo.
